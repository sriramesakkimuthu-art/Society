Say No to Drugs

  • Protects your health, future, and relationships.
  • Build confidence and self-respect.
  • Choose healthy habits, friends, and support.

Types of Drugs

  1. Stimulants – Increase alertness and energy (e.g., cocaine, caffeine, nicotine)
  2. Depressants – Slow down brain function and cause relaxation (e.g., alcohol, sleeping pills)
  3. Hallucinogens – Cause distorted perceptions and hallucinations (e.g., LSD, magic mushrooms)
  4. Opioids (Narcotics) – Used for pain relief; highly addictive (e.g., heroin, morphine)
  5. Cannabis – Has both depressant and hallucinogenic effects (e.g., marijuana)
  6. Inhalants – Chemicals inhaled for a quick high; very harmful (e.g., glue, paint thinner)

    Physical Health Problems

    • Weakens the immune system
    • Causes liver, lung, and heart damage
    • Increases risk of overdose and death

    Mental Health Issues

    • Leads to anxiety, depression, and paranoia
    • Affects memory, thinking, and decision-making
    • Can cause hallucinations and psychosis

    Addiction and Dependency

    • Creates a strong, harmful urge to use the drug
    • Difficult to stop without help
    • Leads to withdrawal symptoms

    Damage to Relationships

    • Breaks trust with family and friends
    • Increases chances of conflict and isolation

    Poor Academic or Work Performance

    • Causes lack of focus and low motivation
    • Leads to absenteeism and failure

    Legal and Social Consequences

    • Involves criminal charges or arrest
    • Affects future opportunities and reputation

    Accidents and Injuries

  1. Increases risk of road accidents and risky behavior
